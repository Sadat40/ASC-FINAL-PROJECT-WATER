Dirty water is water that contains harmful substances, such as bacteria, viruses, parasites, chemicals, and heavy metals. These substances can cause a variety of health problems, including:

  • Diarrhea: Diarrhea is the most common illness caused by dirty water. It is caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites that can make you sick if you drink or eat contaminated water. Diarrhea can cause dehydration, which can be fatal, especially in young children and the elderly.
  • Cholera: Cholera is a serious illness caused by a bacterium that is found in dirty water. It causes severe diarrhea, vomiting, and dehydration. If not treated, cholera can be fatal.
  • Typhoid fever: Typhoid fever is a serious illness caused by a bacterium that is found in dirty water. It causes fever, headache, cough, and fatigue. Typhoid fever can be fatal if not treated with antibiotics.
  • Hepatitis A: Hepatitis A is a liver infection that is caused by a virus that is found in dirty water. It can cause jaundice, fatigue, nausea, and vomiting. Hepatitis A can be prevented by vaccination.
  • Amoebic dysentery: Amebic dysentery is a serious illness caused by a parasite that is found in dirty water. It causes diarrhea, stomach cramps, and blood in the stool. Amebic dysentery can be treated with antibiotics.
  • Giardiasis: Giardiasis is an illness caused by a parasite that is found in dirty water. It causes diarrhea, gas, bloating, and fatigue. Giardiasis can be treated with medication.
  • Ascaris: Ascaris is a parasitic worm that can be found in dirty water. It can cause abdominal pain, diarrhea, and weight loss. Ascaris can be treated with medication.
  • Hookworm: Hookworm is a parasitic worm that can be found in dirty water. It can cause anemia, fatigue, and weight loss. Hookworms can be treated with medication.
  • In addition to these health problems, dirty water can also cause skin problems, eye problems, and respiratory problems. It can also make people more susceptible to other diseases.
